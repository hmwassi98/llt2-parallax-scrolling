Layers of Stone

Rocks tell the story of time in a way few other natural forms can. Every layer, crack, and surface is shaped slowly by wind, pressure, heat, and rain. What appears still and silent often holds the memory of thousands, or even millions, of years.

This parallax design is inspired by rocky landscapes and the depth found in nature. As the user scrolls, the moving layers create the feeling of distance, scale, and uneven terrain. The effect aims to reflect how mountains, cliffs, and stone formations reveal themselves gradually.

In nature, no rock is formed instantly. Stone is built through patience, pressure, and transformation. In the same way, this page is designed to feel like a journey through layers of earth, where each visual element adds to the atmosphere of the scene.

The foreground rocks represent texture and detail, while the background layers suggest vast open space. Together, they create a sense of depth that turns a simple webpage into a more immersive environment. The goal is not only to show movement, but to create the impression of a living landscape.

This project explores how parallax scrolling can be used to present natural scenery in a more engaging way. By combining layered visuals with motion, the page becomes more than a static design — it becomes a slow passage through stone, shadow, and space.
